**Automated Page Turner for Musicians**
Tabone, Bonnici, Cristina — *Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence*, 2020
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7861241/

**Hardware, NOT WebGazer:** They use an **SMI RED500 infrared eye-tracker** (binocular, up to 500 Hz, *built-in* corneal-reflex head-movement compensation tolerant to 50 cm/s, ~0.4° accuracy, ~40×20 cm head box at 60–80 cm). So this paper does NOT corroborate anything about WebGazer's webcam ridge-regression calibration — it's a different tracking stack. What it DOES validate: even pro hardware needs head-compensation + downstream Kalman smoothing; nobody relies on raw calibration alone. See [[gaze-calibration-degradation]].

**Core idea:** Track musician's gaze on the score; auto-scroll/turn when gaze reaches bottom of displayed region. Keeps musician in the loop — turns are paced to actual reading position.

**Key problem solved:** Musicians look away from score toward hands; eye leaves FOV → erratic turns. Fix: **Kalman filter** predicts gaze position during look-away periods.

**Evaluation:** 15 piano songs, varying difficulty, repeats, different registers. 289 page turns total:
- 98.3% correct
- 1.7% delayed
- 0% mistaken

**Relevant concepts for gaze-scroll app:**
- Eye-hand span: musicians read ahead of what they're playing
- Half-page turns (scroll partway, not full page flip)
- Gaze prediction during look-away — Kalman filter is the practical solution
